Strange attractors

Strange attractors in 3d

Velocity field simulations with particles and a solution curve.

For the 3d perspective I used the p5.EasyCam library developed by Thomas Diewald (http://thomasdiewald.com).

The fourth-degree Runge-Kutta method is used to calculate the numerical solutions of each system of non-linear differential equations.

Thomas attractor

Parameter:

$b=0.19$

System:

$$\left\{ \begin{eqnarray*} \frac{dx}{dt}&=&bx+\sin(y)\\ \frac{dy}{dt}&=&-by+\sin(z)\\ \frac{dz}{dt}&=&-bz+\sin(x) \end{eqnarray*}\right. $$

Aizawa attractor

Parameters:

$a = 0.95,$ $b = 0.7,$ $c = 0.6,$ $d = 3.5,$ $e = 0.25,$ $f = 0.1$

System:

$$\left\{ \begin{eqnarray*} \frac{dx}{dt}&=&(z - b) x - d y\\ \frac{dy}{dt}&=& d x + (z - b) y\\ \frac{dz}{dt}&=&c + a z - \frac{z^3}{3} - (x^2+y^2)(1+e z)+f z x^3 \end{eqnarray*}\right. $$

Lorenz attractor

Parameters:

$p = 10,$ $r = 28,$ $b = 8/3$

System:

$$\left\{ \begin{eqnarray*} \frac{dx}{dt}&=&p (-x + y)\\ \frac{dy}{dt}&=& -x z + r x - y \\ \frac{dz}{dt}&=& x y - b z \end{eqnarray*}\right. $$

Dadras attractor

Parameters:

$a = 3,$ $b = 2.7,$ $c = 1.7,$ $d = 2,$ $e = 9$

System:

$$\left\{ \begin{eqnarray*} \frac{dx}{dt}&=& y - a x +b y z\\ \frac{dy}{dt}&=& c y - x z +z\\ \frac{dz}{dt}&=& d x y - e z \end{eqnarray*}\right. $$
